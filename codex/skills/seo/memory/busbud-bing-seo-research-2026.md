# Busbud Bing SEO Research

## Exact Evidence Window
- Bing insights snapshot: 2026-04-13
- Forensic audit report: 2026-04-16

## Core Diagnosis
Bing is showing a brutal concentration pattern on Busbud: a small number of pages drive most value, a very large tail gets zero clicks, and crawl freshness strongly correlates with performance. This is a prioritization and template-framing problem as much as an indexing problem.

## Highest-Signal Numbers
- URLs in scope: about `10.1K`
- Total clicks in the Bing export summary: `15,396`
- Overall CTR in the JSON insight set: about `1.42%`
- Dead pages with zero clicks and zero impressions: `5,219`
- Zero-click URLs: `9,242`
- Zero-backlink URLs: `10,018`
- URLs not crawled in 2026: `8,565`

## What Matters Most
- More than half the set is functionally dead in Bing.
- The top 10 URLs drive about `51.9%` of all clicks.
- The top 50 URLs drive about `80.3%` of all clicks.
- Pages crawled recently account for most actual value, which means stale crawling is not a vanity metric here.

## Locale Readout
- `en` brings most of the scale.
- `en-za` is smaller, but its CTR is materially better.
- The reusable interpretation is that South Africa pages may be framed or matched better when they surface, but they need more discovery and support.

## Reusable Action Logic
- Protect the small set of pages already winning on Bing.
- Rewrite titles and snippets for pages with high impressions and weak CTR.
- Triage the long tail rather than treating every indexed page as equally worth saving.
- Use internal linking and stronger template support on `en-za` winners before defaulting to backlink-heavy explanations.

## Bing-Specific Query For Future Tasks
- Which pages have impressions but zero clicks?
- Which high-impression operator pages underperform on CTR?
- Which stale pages still matter commercially enough to justify crawl-refresh support?
- Which English and South Africa operator twins show the strongest gap and can be used for naming/template comparison?
