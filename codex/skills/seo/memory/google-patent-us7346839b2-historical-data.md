# Core Concept
`US7346839B2` explains how search systems can score documents with historical data such as content updates, link age and growth, user behavior, topic history, and other long-term quality evidence.

## Official Patent Record
- Publication: `US7346839B2`
- Other version: `US20050071741A1`
- Title: `Information retrieval based on historical data`
- Prior art date: `2003-09-30`
- Filing date: `2003-12-31`
- Publication date: `2008-03-18`
- Assignee: `Google LLC`
- Inventors: `Anurag Acharya`, `Matt Cutts`, `Jeffrey Dean`, `Paul Haahr`, `Monika Henzinger`, `Urs Hoelzle`, `Steve Lawrence`, `Karl Pfleger`
- Legal status: `Expired - Lifetime`

## Practical Mechanism
- The patent collects history data for documents and uses it to alter or generate scoring.
- History can include document inception, update frequency, link age, link growth patterns, user behavior, query behavior, and topic changes over time.
- The official record explicitly notes that sudden link growth or sudden topic spikes can indicate spam or doorway behavior.
- Historical data can raise, lower, or otherwise modify relevance scores before results are shown.

## SEO Reading
- Patent Core emphasizes that historical data is not just page age. It is the quality of how a page and its ecosystem evolve over time.
- Freshness is useful when it is substantive. Empty updates are weaker than real maintenance and continued usefulness.
- Natural link acquisition and stable topic development are safer than abrupt manipulative growth.

## What To Reuse
- Maintain important pages with real improvements, not superficial date refreshes.
- Monitor link growth, engagement trends, and topic consistency over time.
- Use this note when diagnosing whether a page lost trust because it stagnated, drifted, or accumulated suspicious growth patterns.
- Pair it with quality, NavBoost, and entity notes for a more complete long-horizon diagnosis.

## Small Details Worth Remembering
- The official patent record includes document inception, content updates, link age, link growth, peer linkage independence, topic changes, user behavior, and query history as valid history inputs.
- It explicitly calls out sudden peer-link growth and sudden topic spikes as possible spam or doorway indicators.
- Historical data can alter relevance scores rather than merely add a side score.
- The patent is broad enough to explain both positive reinforcement and demotion logic.

## E-E-A-T And Audit Lens
- Trust is cumulative. Stable topic ownership, natural link history, and good behavior patterns build durable quality evidence.
- Experience is reflected in real maintenance and continued usefulness over time, not just a publish date.
- Sites that repeatedly shift topic, ownership, link patterns, or engagement quality can look less reliable.

## Real Audit Questions
- Has this page improved substantively over time or only cosmetically?
- Does link growth look organic, gradual, and relevant?
- Is the topic history stable, or did the URL drift into a different purpose?
- Are engagement and selection signals likely improving, flat, or deteriorating?

## Caveat
The patent is old and expired, but the underlying design philosophy remains important because it frames historical reinforcement, freshness, and spam risk as measurable system inputs.
