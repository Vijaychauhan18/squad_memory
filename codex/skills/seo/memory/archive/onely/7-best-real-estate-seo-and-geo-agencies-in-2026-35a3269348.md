1. GEO and AI Search Optimization Capabilities With AI systems increasingly influencing buyer discovery, agencies must demonstrate genuine methodology for optimizing brand visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and similar platforms. 2. Technical SEO Depth for Complex Architectures Enterprise real estate websites face unique challenges: JavaScript-heavy property displays, millions of dynamic listing pages, multi-location hierarchies, and Core Web Vitals issues with image-heavy content. Evaluate capability beyond basic audits—whether agencies can embed in development workflows and build scalable technical frameworks.

3. Customer Journey Intelligence and Strategic Depth Effective real estate SEO/GEO requires understanding the complete buyer journey from initial problem awareness through comparison, decision, and post-purchase—not just targeting high-volume keywords that aggregators dominate. Assess whether agencies map authentic buyer psychology through conversation analysis or simply chase search volume.

4. Enterprise Workflow Integration and Scalability Enterprise real estate companies need agencies that integrate with development teams, work within existing tooling (Jira, sprint planning), and build repeatable frameworks—not agencies designed for SMB clients delivering one-off recommendations that create implementation bottlenecks.

5. Measurement Methodology and ROI Demonstration With GEO being an emerging discipline, agencies must demonstrate rigorous measurement approaches—tracking AI citations, brand mentions, and answer system presence—not just traditional ranking metrics that don’t capture AI search visibility.

6. Aggregator Competition Strategy The dominant challenge in real estate SEO is competing with Zillow, Redfin, and similar aggregators. Evaluate whether agencies demonstrate strategic thinking about where competition is viable and how GEO creates alternative competitive opportunities rather than promising unrealistic outcomes.
