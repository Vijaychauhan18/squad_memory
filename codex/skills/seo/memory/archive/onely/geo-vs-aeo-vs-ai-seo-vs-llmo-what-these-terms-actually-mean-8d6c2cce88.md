## Extracted Body
Here’s what each means, where they overlap, and how to evaluate vendors using these terms.

GEO was formally defined in November 2023 – less than two years ago – by Princeton University researchers . LLMO emerged from practitioner communities around the same time. AEO predates both but has been repurposed for AI contexts. The terminology is new, unstandardized, and applied inconsistently across the industry.

This matters because you’re not confused due to a knowledge gap. You’re confused because the industry itself hasn’t agreed on definitions.

Consider the professional landscape: 37% of SEO professionals admit they don’t know how to use AI tools. If the experts are uncertain, executive confusion is entirely rational.

Meanwhile, 73% of agency leaders acknowledge AI has radically transformed search – yet agencies define and apply these terms differently, making vendor comparison genuinely difficult.

Click-through rates for top-ranking results dropped from 28.5% to 18.5% following AI Overview expansion – a 32-35% decline .

You’re being asked to master new terminology while watching established metrics deteriorate.

GEO is the practice of optimizing content to be cited by AI systems like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google Gemini.

The term emerged from academic research at Princeton in November 2023. GEO focuses on creating comprehensive, authoritative content that generative AI selects as source material when synthesizing answers.

For non-technical executives: GEO determines whether ChatGPT mentions your company when someone asks about your category.

Practitioners are actively debating the relationship between traditional SEO and GEO.

AEO optimizes content to appear in featured snippets, voice assistant responses, and zero-click search results.

AEO predates generative AI, originally targeting Google’s featured snippets and voice search. According to PwC and Conductor , AEO prioritizes concise, structured Q&A content using conversational language.

For non-technical executives: AEO determines whether your content appears in the answer box above traditional search results.

AI SEO is the umbrella term for integrating artificial intelligence into search optimization strategy.

According to Salesforce and Search Engine Land , AI SEO encompasses using AI tools for content optimization, keyword research automation, predictive analytics, and adapting to AI-powered search features.

For non-technical executives: AI SEO describes any use of AI in search strategy – both the tools you use and the AI systems you optimize for.

LLMO specifically optimizes content for visibility in responses from large language models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

According to Search Atlas and Conductor , LLMO builds topical authority through semantically clear, interlinked content that LLMs recognize as authoritative.

For non-technical executives: LLMO is functionally similar to GEO but originated from practitioners rather than academics, with specific emphasis on LLM platforms.

GEO and LLMO share approximately 80% functional overlap. Both optimize for AI citation.

The distinction is primarily origin – GEO from academia, LLMO from practitioners – and scope.

GEO encompasses all generative engines; LLMO specifically targets large language models.

AEO differs more meaningfully. While GEO and LLMO optimize for conversational AI responses, AEO primarily targets traditional search interfaces – featured snippets, voice results, answer boxes.

AEO content tends toward concise Q&A pairs. GEO and LLMO content tends toward comprehensive depth.

AI SEO contains them all. Any agency offering GEO, AEO, or LLMO is technically offering a subset of AI SEO.

The term also covers using AI tools for traditional SEO tasks – content generation, keyword research, competitive analysis.

This ambiguity isn’t necessarily deceptive – the terminology genuinely lacks consensus.

But it means you need to ask outcome-based questions rather than accepting terminology at face value.

The urgency behind this terminology shift comes from measurable performance changes.

These numbers explain why your SEO investment may be delivering declining returns despite maintained spend. The market shifted. Your agency’s execution may be fine – the channel itself is changing.

You don’t need to become an expert in these acronyms. You need to ask questions that reveal whether vendors understand what they’re actually offering.

“Which AI systems are you optimizing for – ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, voice assistants, or others? How does your approach differ for each?”

Why it works: Vendors with genuine expertise can articulate platform-specific strategies. Those rebranding traditional SEO will give vague answers.

“How do you track whether our content is being cited by AI systems? What tools do you use, and what metrics will you report?”

Why it works: AI citation measurement is genuinely difficult. Vendors should acknowledge this complexity and explain their approach honestly.

“Where in our customers’ research process do AI systems most influence their decisions? How would you prioritize optimization across those touchpoints?”

Why it works: This shifts from terminology to strategic thinking. It reveals whether the vendor understands your business context or just sells generic services.

“Can you show examples of client content being cited in AI responses? What was your approach, and how did you track the result?”

Why it works: Case studies with verifiable outcomes separate proven capability from theoretical knowledge.

The skepticism from experienced practitioners is worth noting.

“I’m convinced there’s no method yet. Anyone who promises to rank you on ChatGPT is just selling air. LLMs don’t index the web in real time, they learn from a combination of curated data and verified sources.

That’s why your Google rankings don’t automatically translate into visibility through AI.

The best thing you can do is stay present in respected publications and maintain consistency in your brand language.”

Terminology mastery matters less than understanding where AI intersects your customer journey.
