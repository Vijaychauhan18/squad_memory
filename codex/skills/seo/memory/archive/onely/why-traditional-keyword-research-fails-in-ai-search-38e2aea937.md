# Why Traditional Keyword Research Fails in AI Search

Source: Onely
Homepage: https://www.onely.com/blog/

## Summary
Learn how AI Search impact organic traffic and why shifting to Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) drives better B2B business outcomes.

## Extracted Body
Traditional keyword research fails in AI search because the entire methodology was built for a system that no longer exists.

And the queries users submit to AI systems – averaging 23 words versus 3-4 words in traditional search – don’t match keyword-optimized content at all.

The result: Marketing teams hit every SEO target while business outcomes decline. If your dashboard shows green and your pipeline shows red, you’re not alone. 37.1% of B2B SaaS websites experienced traffic decline in 2024-2025 despite maintaining or improving their rankings.

This isn’t a tactical failure. It’s a structural shift that requires a different approach entirely.

Keyword research tools have significant accuracy problems that most teams never see.

A study comparing SEMrush traffic estimates against actual Google Search Console data across 184 websites revealed:

The problem compounds for B2B companies. These tools rely on predictive models using keyword rankings and CTR data – models that break down for niche audiences with limited search volume. Most B2B websites fall exactly into this low-traffic profile where the tools perform worst.

When your strategic decisions rest on data with coin-flip accuracy, keyword research becomes expensive guesswork.

“When there were 10 blue links on Google, Semrush and Ahrefs had it easy. Now things are more complicated with AI etc, and neither of them can produce anything much better than GSC. It’s all smoke and mirrors now, dressed up as data.”

AI Overviews now appear in 31-47% of Google searches, and they’re cannibalizing clicks from every position.

The mechanism is straightforward: AI systems synthesize answers from multiple sources and present them above organic results. Users get what they need without scrolling. 60% of US searches in 2024 ended without any click , according to TNG Shopper . That number is projected to hit 70% by mid-2025.

This breaks the historical correlation between rankings and traffic. Your team can achieve every traditional SEO objective – ranking #1, growing keyword portfolio, increasing domain authority – while simultaneously losing the visibility that actually drives business outcomes.

“In the recent months, one of our top performing websites’ visits decreased by 66%. And after some investigation, we noticed everything is going well. We still have the same positions and the same click through rates. However, the only issue we see is that websites is not getting searches, it dropped by like more than 50%. When we search for it, we still see it on top like normal.”

Search volume and business value have an inverse relationship in B2B.

The assumption underlying keyword research – that higher volume means higher value – runs directly contrary to B2B conversion data:

High-volume keywords attract browsers. Low-volume keywords attract buyers.

When your keyword research methodology systematically prioritizes the keywords that generate traffic without conversions, it actively works against business outcomes. Volume-based prioritization isn’t just outdated – it was always the wrong metric for B2B.

B2B purchasing involves 6-15 stakeholders searching at different times with different intent. Volume metrics can’t capture this.

A single monthly search volume number collapses the complexity of B2B buying into a meaningless average. The champion researches solutions. The technical evaluator searches for integration requirements. The CFO looks for ROI benchmarks. The procurement team checks compliance certifications.

Each search happens at different points in a sales cycle that spans months. Each represents a different buying signal. But keyword research treats them all as interchangeable search volume – missing the decision-making dynamics that actually drive purchases.

B2B sales cycles in complex scenarios extend 1-2 quarters or more. The relationship between any single search query and actual purchase intent is attenuated across stakeholders and time. A keyword spike might indicate initial exploration by one person while the actual purchase decision happens weeks later with minimal search activity.

Volume-based keyword research was designed for consumer purchases where one person searches, clicks, and buys. It was never appropriate for B2B – AI search just made this more obvious.

AI systems match on intent and context, not keywords. Content optimized for phrase matching may be invisible to systems matching on meaning.

Traditional search queries average 3-4 words. AI search queries average 23 words – nearly 5x longer. These aren’t keyword strings. They’re conversational questions that express complex, multi-layered intent.

Over 70% of AI search queries don’t fit traditional intent categories (informational, transactional, navigational). They’re task-oriented and conversational – categories that keyword research frameworks don’t address.

AI-powered intent matching achieves up to 85% accuracy in identifying user intent, compared to 60-70% for keyword-based systems. AI uses semantic search and contextual analysis to understand meaning even when exact keywords aren’t present.

The implication: Content can be keyword-perfect and still not selected by AI systems matching on intent rather than terms.

Ranking #1 on Google means appearing in AI search answers only 25% of the time.

Research analyzing 25,000 user searches found that websites ranked #1 on Google appear in AI answers only 25% of the time .

Strong SEO rankings can coexist with minimal AI visibility because AI systems use different selection criteria.

While 76% of AI Overview citations come from pages in Google’s top 10 , traditional ranking is necessary but not sufficient. AI systems weigh brand signals more heavily than backlinks or domain authority – the metrics that keyword research traditionally optimized toward.

The result: A content team can achieve every traditional SEO objective while losing visibility in the environment where users increasingly find answers.

The Stratabeat B2B SaaS SEO Performance Report found that 37.1% of B2B SaaS websites experienced traffic decline in 2024-2025 despite maintaining rankings . Companies with stable or improved keyword positions saw significant drops in actual organic traffic.

Even industry leaders aren’t immune. The Digital Bloom reports HubSpot – the company that literally invented inbound marketing – saw 70-80% decline in organic traffic between 2024 and 2025 despite maintained rankings.

If HubSpot is experiencing this, your company can’t SEO its way out.

Marketing professionals across industries are seeing the same pattern.

“Clicks are nose diving, impressions higher than ever. This is the new organic”

AI search traffic converts at 5x the rate of traditional organic traffic.

Semrush confirms this pattern : AI search users convert 4.4x better than traditional search users.

This reframes the traffic decline. When visitors who do arrive convert at 5x the rate and generate 67% higher lifetime value, raw traffic volume becomes a misleading metric.

Declining traffic may mask improving traffic quality – the visitors you’re losing were never going to buy anyway.

Brands in the top 25% for web mentions get 10x more AI visibility than others. The top 50 brands account for 28.9% of all AI Overview citations. Track your brand mentions across web properties and benchmark against competitors.

Monitor whether your content appears in AI search responses for relevant queries. Sources cited by answer engines see 27% higher click-through rates than traditional placements. This is more manual than rank tracking but more accurate for AI visibility.

Segment traffic by source. Track AI search traffic separately. Evaluate on conversion and engagement rather than volume. When 56% of B2B marketers struggle to attribute ROI to content marketing, the reporting framework itself is the problem.

Move beyond traffic to pipeline and revenue. Leads generated, opportunities influenced, deals closed – these connect search investment to business outcomes in ways keyword rankings cannot.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) optimizes for AI citation rather than keyword rankings.

GEO is the practice of optimizing content to be cited by AI search systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude. Unlike traditional SEO which optimizes for keyword rankings, GEO optimizes for citation likelihood based on brand authority, content structure, and semantic relevance.
