For almost 20 years, the goal of the Hobo SEO blog has been to provide SEO guidance that is, as one reader kindly put it, “conjecture-free.” I’ve always aimed to deliver “boring things but real things,” grounded in deep research and two decades of real-world experience.

The events of the last year – namely, the landmark U.S. v. Google trial and the unprecedented Google API leak – have changed the very nature of our industry. The “black box” has been cracked open, and the foundational rules of search have been revealed.

In response, I haven’t just updated my old guides; I’ve written a completely new, four-part library for this new, evidence-based era. Each book is built from the ground up on a foundation of verifiable, primary-source evidence, designed to provide a complete education for every level of expertise.

These works are unique works of mine and a form of logical, creative inference optimisation – where theory and blueprint meet – and build on the seminal leak work of Mike King, Rand Fishkin and many others over 20 years.

I use Gemini Pro 2.5/3 to complete almost all my writing (I use it as a copy editor and an organiser of my stream of thoughts).

As Martin MacDonald of Web Marketing School (WMS) so generously put it, I have a “solid, solid multi-decade history of being insightful, ahead of the curve, pragmatic and good to interact with to boot”.

This collection is the culmination of that history, synthesised for the new reality of search and the first books to do so.

The Hobo SEO Quadrilogy , published on 22 October 2025, “a groundbreaking collection of four ebooks by Shaun Anderson,” is free to download.

If you’re new to SEO, the industry can feel like a minefield of conflicting advice and complex jargon.

I wrote this book to be your safe and accessible on-ramp, designed to start your journey with the correct, durable principles from day one.

This is the strategic heart of the collection. It’s the definitive playbook for the professional who needs to translate deep technical realities into winning business strategies.

As fellow SEO Charles Floate said, this eBook “looks like an absolute banger,” and for good reason. It’s designed to make, as Daniel J. Pinna noted, “a rather complex situation easy to understand for anyone.”

The future of search isn’t about ranking a list of blue links; it’s about becoming the trusted source for AI-driven answer engines.

I wrote this forward-looking guide for the strategist who isn’t just playing today’s game but is preparing for the next one.

This is the masterwork of the collection – a deep, exhaustive dive into the core architecture of Google’s ranking systems.

It is not a book for everyone; it is a reference manual for the true specialists who need to understand the fundamental mechanics of the machine.

This is my art . Thank you for being on this journey with me.
