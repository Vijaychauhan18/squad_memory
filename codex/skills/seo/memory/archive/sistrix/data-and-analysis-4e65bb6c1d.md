### Data and Analysis

The SISTRIX Update radar has shown large amounts of background change in recent weeks but using our extended daily data we are able to view the domains that have risen or fallen significantly during the Core Update.

#### UK – Winners and Losers

First, a look at the general trend across sectors. This graphic is based on the top 600 winning and losing established domains and is a good representation of what happened across sectors.

For example, across news media sites there was average absolute VI gain (more click potential), larger than the gain seen across the video gaming sites. Conversely losses were seen across informational media and education sites.

As always this is a zero-sum calculation when all (4m +) domains are taken into account. Wins are taken when other sites lose. Either losses and gains have occured between sectors listed above or there have been movements spread widely across other, smaller and perhaps more focused domains.

#### Machine analysis

In parallel with our human-led data journalism processes we also applied a multi-stage AI analysis to SISTRIX data. Here is the summary of findings

Authority beats interchangeability. This is the central message of the March 2026 Core Update in the UK, mirroring the pattern we found in Germany, France, Spain and Italy.

Official brand shops gain at the expense of generic retailers. NHS trusts gain while third-party health content loses. The update rewards domains that are the natural first port of call for their topic.

The losses among reference sites and knowledge databases (Oxford Reference, Animal Diversity Web, convertunits.com, tvtropes.org) deserve attention. These sites offer content that is increasingly covered by AI-generated answers in search. Whether Google actively devalues them or whether this reflects a broader shift in search behaviour is a question the coming updates will answer.

### Daily updates

#### 31 March 2026 – No major movements across UK SERPS

The radar (updated, above) does not show any uptick in activity for this update yet but that is due to large amounts of background movement potentially masking the effects of this Core Update. There are, however, no absolute changes in the top 100 domains so the update is not be having a huge effect across the board.

Domain data from 31st March does reveal some candidate domains indicating that the update has started but in terms of established domains with a Visibility Index of over 1, there aren’t many examples.

There are no clear winners among established (VI >1) domains at this point.

Note that motors.co.uk is migrating to cazoo.co.uk in the UK right now so there may be some movements among car-related domains that are not related to the core update.

#### 1st April 2026 – No major movements seen

### Summary of findings for the March 2026 Core Update

The absolute size of the March Core Update wasn’t big in relation both to previous core updates and to the background level of change occurring across the SERPs at the time.

There’s a common agreement amoing SEOs, our data journalism team and through machine analysis, that informational content was impacted negatively by the March Core Update. This impact comes at a time when AIOs are drastically reducing the chance of a click while expanding their coverage, especially in informational searches.

News Media gained in general but because the levels of change were not that big, publishers may not see this as a game-changing event.
