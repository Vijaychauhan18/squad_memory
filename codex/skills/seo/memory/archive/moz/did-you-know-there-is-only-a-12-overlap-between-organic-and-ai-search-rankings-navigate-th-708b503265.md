### Standard organic search and AI Mode are worlds apart

You can’t assume that ranking well in traditional search automatically gets you a spot in AI search. In fact, there is hardly any overlap between organic rankings and AI Mode citations.

> "We only see a 12% overlap between the top 10 URLs featured in the regular organic and the top 10 featured URLs cited in the AI Mode rankings."

For anyone ranking in the bottom half of the SERPs, it's not great news. AI mode is selecting slightly higher-ranked URLs from a broader set of queries, rather than selecting the full top 10 for a single query. This is because of the fan-out functionality, which we'll learn more about further down in this post.

### How to build a 6-Step AI content workflow that preserves your voice

Top-of-the-funnel content remains an important part of your organic search strategy, especially for specific intents and industries . While you might not be able to measure results with a single click or a single click that converts anymore, high-quality, authoritative content builds brand trust, earns AI citations, and serves as a strategic asset for acquiring and distributing link equity to key product pages. But importantly, in a world of AI fluff and AI slop, you shouldn't use AI to write your content, but to support your writing game plan.

> "Use AI, but not to generate the whole article. Use it to refine ideas, add metaphors, add examples, do the research, get the outline, but inject that with your original ideas."

### Mastering query fan-outs for AI search

AI search results are an important part of the buyer journey, whether it's an AI Overview in Google search or a response to your prompt about different ways to repair a dishwasher pump. Google is searching for more than what you typed. It's called query fan-out, and it runs dozens of related searches behind the scenes to help build a complete topic in anticipation of your next query.

Understanding these categories helps you anticipate your audience's needs more effectively and create content that appeals to them, LLMs, and search engines.

### AI search starts with a prompt

Identify prompts that matter to your brand with Prompt Suggestions in Moz Pro.

SEOs and brands can influence their visibility in AI-generated answers by proactively building citations. You can reverse-engineer AI responses to identify which publications are being cited and then use proactive outreach to secure brand mentions in those sources

Charlie Marchant explains that, since AI platforms like ChatGPT and Gemini cite specific sources, marketers should research which prompts lead to those citations, then reach out to those publications to gain brand mentions and contextual relevance.

> "More and more people are actually starting their buyer journey in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and other AI platforms. ChatGPT alone has around 2.5 billion searches every day. And perhaps we care a little bit about the demographics of who is actually searching in those platforms."

### Stop treating AI like a magic engine: Practical advice to smarter AI workflows

I'm leaving you with one last resource, which was personally a delight to be part of, and I think provides a fantastic palate cleanser for all the workflows, strategies, and urgency we see in the industry.

> "Britney emphasizes that to use AI effectively, you have to be willing to embrace failure because it's the 'yellow brick road of using AI.' The process is inherently experimental and should be treated as such. She advises thinking like an engineer, which means breaking down problems into small, manageable chunks and being specific in your prompts. Avoid the "magic engine" mentality where you provide a vague prompt and expect a perfect result."
