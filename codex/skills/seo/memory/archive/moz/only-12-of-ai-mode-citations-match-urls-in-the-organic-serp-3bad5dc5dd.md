# Only 12% of AI Mode Citations Match URLs in the Organic SERP

Source: Moz Blog
Homepage: https://moz.com/blog
Original URL: https://moz.com/blog/ai-mode-citations
Published: 2026-02-18
Strength: SEO education, beginner-to-advanced resources, brand/entity SEO, whiteboard-style explainers

## Summary
Why do only 12% of AI Mode citations match organic SERP URLs? Discover how AI Mode selects sources and how to win visibility beyond rankings.

## Extracted Body
The data for this study was collected using STAT — you can see the full data here .

Google’s AI Mode is introducing new patterns in how search results are assembled. While traditional rankings still matter, it’s not the only route to organic visibility.

This report analyzes nearly 40,000 search queries to uncover the biggest winners of AI Mode citations and how often overlap occurs with traditional SERPs.

To understand how AI Mode generates results and selects citations, we analyzed a structured dataset that included nearly 40,000 queries across desktop and mobile in the US and the UK.

Each AI Mode query returns up to 38 results, with data on type (paragraph, list, etc.), traits (citation, answer, etc.), position, and layout structure.

Traditional SERPs for the keywords were extracted to measure overlap with AI Mode citations.

There’s not a lot of overlap between AI Mode citations and Google’s top 10 results for the same query — just as we’d expect given the fan out methodology.

Only 1 in 10 AI citations match the exact URLs in Google’s top 10 organic results. Even when compared at the domain level, just 1 in 5 citations come from the same websites that appear in the top 10 for that query. When overlap happens, it’s concentrated near the top, but still far from guaranteed.

“AI Mode is branching out to a broader set of queries and topics rather than just the exact one you typed in... It makes these sort of ‘fan-out’ queries and aggregates results.”

In simple terms, AI Mode runs multiple related searches behind the scenes (e.g., variations, subtopics, adjacent intents). It then aggregates citations from those related queries, not just from the top results of the original query.

In this dataset, a higher Google rank clearly increases the odds of being cited in AI Mode, but even the #1 result is only cited some of the time.

For most queries, while there is some shared sourcing between AI Mode and organic results, the citation list is mostly broader than the SERP because AI Mode uses a wider set of queries.

The majority of AI Mode answers pull from a small set of highly trusted domains, probably due to their prominent positions in the top few organic results for the fan-out queries.

The top four domains account for 10% of all citations, signalling the sources AI Mode prefers when generating responses.

Expand your citation surface area. UGC domains like Reddit, YouTube, Facebook, and LinkedIn appear frequently. If your brand is absent or poorly represented on those ecosystems, you may lose mindshare even if your site ranks.

Include video production in your content strategy. YouTube ranks second among the most cited domains in AI Mode. That means high-quality video content is a core part of how the model builds trustworthy answers.

AI Mode is citation-heavy by design. Out of nearly 40,000 responses analyzed, 96% included at least one citation.

This clearly contradicts the assumption that AI Mode skips attribution. On the contrary, it’s citation-heavy, often pulling multiple URLs into the summary even when it looks minimal on the surface.

With 91% of responses citing 10 or more sources, AI Mode behaves more like a bibliography than a top‑10 list. However, the goal isn’t just to get your site cited, but to earn a mention in the paragraphs that appear on already-cited domains. Focus on being part of the answer, wherever it appears.

Pages that get cited tend to cover a specific micro-intent. Avoid generic overviews and ensure your pages focus on a single, well-defined intent.

According to John Iwuozor , “AI Mode processes content at the passage level, pulling out modular chunks to build its responses.”

Since AI Mode is pulling text-based citations, structure your content with clear subheadings and focused paragraphs. Make it easy for the model to identify what your page answers and where it fits in the bigger picture.

AI Mode doesn’t rely on a single search result. As Tom Capper explains, it runs multiple related queries behind the scenes and builds answers from a broader intent set.
