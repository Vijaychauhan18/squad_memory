## Extracted Body
Podcasts that I’ll return to repeatedly don't just have decent audio or clear video. They don't just bring on informed guests and cover topics that intrigue and provide perspective. Although all these things are critical. There is something else that sets them apart, and I think I’ve boiled it down to having a little bit of heart. Yes, I want to learn about SEO campaigns and the amazing conversion results these tactics achieve, but I also want to learn about the lives of the people working in SEO. Because it’s relatable, and relatability helps the lessons and learnings stick. Here are the very best podcasts in SEO and digital marketing. Podcasts that have heart and tell stories of SEO and the people in the industry. They keep you informed when you’re working from home or the only SEO at your company. Need a new SEO pod to kick-start your day or jump-start a task? Then read on.

Of course, it wouldn’t be a list that we endorsed without including our very own MozPod. Relaunched in 2024, produced by me and hosted by Chima Mmeje, MozPod tells the stories of people working in SEO. We interview agency founders, consultants, and SEO practitioners to learn more about their experiences working in SEO in 2024. Featuring real-world case studies, like generating $1M monthly e-commerce revenue for a brick-and-mortar brand, insights into the challenges of SEO agency founders, and deep dives into some fun marketing campaigns like the Crocodile Dundee fake ad campaign. MozPod host Chima Mmeje asks guests about their ‘tiny pocket of joy,’ which exposes the real lives, joys, and challenges of the people working in SEO.

Watch the talk show-style video on YouTube or listen on Spotify during your daily commute.

Living up to their tagline “Optimizing your SEO Career,” Sarah and Tazmin are emerging as the podcast queens for SEO and mental health. They bring a comforting, thoughtful arm around the shoulders of anyone slogging in the field of SEO and seeking guidance or coaching. They aren’t afraid to go deep to help SEOs better understand themselves and the challenges facing working in the search industry today. Set aside 40 minutes to soak up the love. Pro tip: the SEO Mindset team record a live session at the fringe events at Brighton SEO, sharing personal sorties and fielding questions from the audience.

The team that gave Wix credibility in the SEO space covers a wide range of topics, from Google updates to international SEO. For a smallish 45-minute investment, you’ll join your next team sync well-informed and grinning from ear to ear. I will never get bored of the dynamic between Mordy and Crystal, or as I call them, my SEO Mom and Dad.

Jack and Mark have created the same vibe as what it would be like sitting down with a few mates or colleages in the pub after work. They provide real world examples and talk through the latest industry news. If you don’t get to lean over and tap another SEO on the shoulder to ask a question, check out these guys that you’d certainly go for a pint with or launch a DnD campaign. I enjoyed their recent episode, Is SearchGPT Better Than Google , and was pleased to see my colleague Tom Capper’s recent article on his findings from the Helpful Content Update research discussed with genuine insight and balance. It really is like well informed office chat that you don’t get working from home. With well-produced audio and well-designed video footage, you could comfortably listen to this on Spotify or watch it on YouTube.

Hailed as the longest-running SEO podcast these guys talk from experience about the challenges they are facing, covering the latest news and providing clear, actionable guidance. Aiming not to overwhelm people starting out in SEO, these guys talk from experience about the challenges they are facing, covering the news with clear guidance. The hosts have good chemistry, and they’ve been at it a while, fast approaching their 500th episode. If you’re just starting out and you only have 30 minutes to level up, it's definitely worth a listen.

Grab your hot cuppa cha and spend 30 minutes in the glow of SEOs Getting Coffee as they talk about Google updates, well-being, and SEO reporting, among other topics. Emina and Sean have created a warm and welcoming space on the web. The production value is really good, especially for a virtual recorded format, which can suffer from audio challenges with latency. The hosts do a great job of creating content that is nice to listen to to watch on YouTube. Emina wrote an article for the Moz Blog about the Sins of an Agency , which is a testament to her openness to sharing the real-world challenges of SEO agency life.

With over 350 episodes, Gael and Mark focus on being practical and actionable, and they sure do deliver. There are a lot of episodes in the backlog, so you’ll never be alone on the daily commute, and you’ll be sure to find the topics that suit you. The average episode length is about an hour, and a personal highlight is Google update coverage and answering practical SEO questions.

I defy you to dislike Claire Carlile. She told me recently when we were filming a Whiteboard Friday (and I don’t think it’s a closely held secret) that she used to be a teacher. And boy, does she bring all that Claire-branded upbeat joy to her pod, Adventures in Local Marketing. Topics tend to focus on local SEO but often extend beyond local into broader topics. My particular favorite is Marie Haynes's Embracing the AI Future .

With the same name as her newsletter, Marie Haynes’ established podcast Search News You Can Use does what it says on the tin. Marie is a knowledgeable contributor to SEO perspective and someone I’ll always pay attention to. Guesting on many other podcasts, I always appreciate her take on industry news, which goes beyond outlining what happened and explaining why it matters and how it affects the day-to-day lives of those working in SEO.

The team at Exposure Ninja chat about all the things you might talk about with your colleagues if you weren’t WFH. Tune in for office chit-chat that keeps you in the SEO loop, covering a wide range of digital marketing topics with a focus on real-world applications. Unique angle: the pod combines insights from a successful agency owner with practical tips for businesses of all sizes. It’s an easy time investment to accompany your lunch break with 20-40 minute episodes. Tim joined us in the 12th episode of MozPod this year and his focus on bringing value and ROI for these clients really shone through.

Search Engine Journal Show’s extensive backlog is worth a look.
