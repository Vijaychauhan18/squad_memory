# 2025 SEO Trends: Top Predictions from 23 Industry Experts

## Summary
Looking for guidance to create an SEO strategy in 2025? Discover the top SEO trends with predictions from 23 search experts.

## Extracted Body
There were many times in 2024 when SEO felt like a telenovela.

Google introduced AI Overviews, which was basically featured snippets 2.0. The Google Leaks confirmed what we’ve long suspected about how Google ranks content. And just as we were coming up for air, we discovered parasite SEO and how big brands have used it to own massive slices of SERPs.

So, how do we prepare for search in 2025? What should we prioritize to reduce reliance on volatile SERPs and future-proof our strategies?

In this article, 23 SEO and content experts share SEO trends and predictions for 2025 to help you prepare for potential changes and chart a course for the future.

Aleyda Solis — International SEO Consultant & Founder at Orainti

These changes will require brands to rethink traditional SEO strategies . Building brand recognition, optimizing product detail pages, and closely tracking visibility in search features will be essential to staying competitive in a changing landscape.

In 2025, SEOs will shift their focus from search rankings to ensuring brand visibility in AI-generated responses.

AI tools are reshaping how users find information, making brand visibility in AI responses essential. While new metrics and practices will emerge, familiar SEO tactics like growing your digital footprint will remain key to ensuring relevance and authority.

Zoe Ashbridge — Co-Founder & Senior SEO Strategist at forank

Earning visibility in AI overviews and generative AI tools will grow in importance in 2025 as users trust these platforms more.

AI is evolving rapidly, and while it’s not yet a major threat to websites, its influence will expand as users increasingly rely on AI insights. To remain visible, brands must adapt strategies to align with how AI processes and presents information, ensuring they meet user needs and AI trust criteria .

In 2025, widespread AI adoption and the global rollout of AI overviews will increase zero-click searches, making it harder for brands to build recognition .

AI responses can’t fully address the varied user intentions across the buying journey, sustaining the need for diverse content strategies. However, Google’s push to keep users in transactional search results will require brands to invest in tools like Google Merchant Center, optimized product feeds, and Schema markup.

At the same time, diversifying beyond organic search into platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and email will be crucial to align with evolving user engagement trends.

In 2025, AI search will push marketers to focus on curated content that blends insights from multiple high-quality sources, delivering real value and resonating with increasingly selective audiences.

As AI better aggregates information, generic content will lose its value. To stand out, brands must create content that integrates diverse perspectives and demonstrates a deep understanding of their audience.

Authenticity will become critical, with real quotes, expert insights, and user-generated perspectives carrying more weight in AI search results.

In 2025, social search will play an even bigger role in search as users increasingly turn to content creators and peers for advice and reviews. Brands that diversify content and nurture communities will win.

As Lily Ray noted during her Moz webinar , “I’m noticing that I go to TikTok or Instagram for reviews rather than Google. I want firsthand insights from small creators because they feel more trustworthy than big brands.”

AI content is making search more fragmented, with users seeking trustworthy advice on platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube. Meanwhile, volatile SERPs and AI overviews are eating into organic traffic, forcing brands to reduce dependence on traditional SEO.

I predict that 2025 will be the year brands focus on diversifying content strategies to reduce dependence on Google and safeguard against algorithm volatility.

This past year has shown how risky it is to rely on Google for traffic, as algorithm updates can devastate a brand’s bottom line. In 2025, brands must diversify their strategies to reduce this dependency and build resilience.

I believe that the traditional SEO role will phase out into a broader "organic growth" role focused on growing multiple organic channels. Platforms like YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, newsletters, and podcasts will offer opportunities to engage new audiences, maximize content value, and create stronger, more engaged communities.

In 2025, SEOs will shift focus from optimizing solely for Google to embracing multiple search engines as new AI platforms emerge.

With tools like SearchGPT and other LLM-based search engines gaining traction, the dominance of Google could wane. Brands must establish credibility, authenticity, and expertise to stand out across diverse search platforms. SEO will become less about "Googling" and more about optimizing for wherever users search.

In 2025, Google will accelerate its AI features, expanding ads and product listings in AI overviews. I also predict that Google will phase out featured snippets as AI overviews become the go-to solution for contextual answers.

With AI overviews gaining prominence, traditional search features like featured snippets may no longer be necessary. Google’s emphasis on AI for product recommendations and contextual answers will change how content surfaces in search results. Brands must adapt by understanding how AI processes queries and source answers to ensure their content remains relevant.

In 2025, Google will improve its product grids, rich results, and commerce-focused search features, becoming a de facto category page for retailers.

As Google continues prioritizing e-commerce search results, competition for visibility will intensify. Retailers must adapt by optimizing their product pages and leveraging tools like product feeds and schema markup. Monitoring performance against third-party retailers will be crucial to maintaining an edge in an increasingly crowded space.

I predict Google will shift its algorithm to leave a little more room for topically focused sites, as opposed to big brands dominating irrelevant search results.

Affiliates and product reviews remain sensitive areas for Google, especially with Amazon's growing competition. Small, topical sites that rank well now may have an opportunity to thrive as Google continues penalizing strategies like Site Reputation Abuse policies, often affecting larger brands.

Nonetheless, if you’ve been affected by the Helpful Content Update (HCU), don’t count on a complete recovery—other channels are still safer bets.

By 2025, brands that excel will master authentic dialogue and thought leadership using content that AI can’t replicate. With AI handling the basics, the winners will stand out by showcasing unique voices and genuine expertise through storytelling.

AI has already eliminated the need for “good enough” content, as competitors can now generate perfectly optimized material at scale. The only advantage left is creating what AI cannot: authentic, unscripted moments that resonate with real human experiences.

Formats like podcasts and conversational content will dominate, offering top-of-funnel value that builds brand affinity. Additionally, while SERP rankings remain in flux, zero-click results provide new opportunities for brands to gain visibility if they maintain a strong voice and creative campaigns.

I believe 2025 will be the year brands rediscover the principles of trust and reciprocity in content marketing . By treating content as a gift, you can rebuild relationships with your audience, strengthen community ties, and create meaningful, lasting connections.

Content marketing has lost its way, focusing on metrics that prioritize algorithms over audience value. This approach erodes trust and fosters behaviors born out of disappointment. Great content should build relationships by being valuable, thoughtful, and aligned with brand values.

Generative AI provides new opportunities to create audience-focused experiences, but only if brands embrace a service mindset and offer content that feels meaningful rather than transactional.

SEO in 2025 will prioritize authentic, human-written content while integrating AI for advanced optimization.

Google prioritizes content showcasing expertise and unique perspectives, moving away from generic, mass-produced copy. At the same time, AI tools are becoming more powerful and capable of handling complex tasks.

It has created opportunities for SEO teams to prioritize content centered on human experiences to build trust with your audience.

In 2025, successful marketers will shift from content creators to story finders, focusing on uncovering and sharing the unique stories their brands are best equipped to tell.

As AI-generated content floods the internet, standing out does not require more content. Instead, marketers must embrace curation, storytelling, and audience engagement to rise above the noise.

Thinking like documentary makers rather than traditional content marketers allows brands to explore meaningful ideas and foster deeper connections with their audiences.

I believe that in 2025, content featuring relatable humans—videos, podcasts, and interviews—will have a far greater impact than AI content.

As AI overview and tools like SearchGPT grow, users will prefer human-focused formats like videos, podcasts, and interviews. SEO will shift from keywords to entities and concept relationships, making brand-building and psychology-led strategies essential for success.

I believe the brands that stand out in 2025 will be those that treat their websites as powerful conversion engines filled with experience-led content that AI can’t easily replicate.

We’ve seen how relying solely on Google can backfire, especially with the rise of AI overviews and updates like the Helpful Content Update .
