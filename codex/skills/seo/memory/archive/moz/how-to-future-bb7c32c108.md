Enter Relevance Engineering, a new framework that blends semantic SEO, topically deep and citation-worthy content architecture, and AI comprehension.

Relevance Engineering is the evolution of semantic SEO tailored for LLM-based retrieval, interpretation, and content summarization. A term originating from iPullRank's massively impressive article on Relevance Engineering and the future of search , it can officially be defined as: "Relevance Engineering is the art and science of improving visibility for any search surface. It’s the intersection of information retrieval, user experience, artificial intelligence, content strategy, and digital PR to give visibility in any form of search." Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on building authority for specific web pages, Relevance Engineering isn't susceptible to "get ranked quick" schemes and general manipulation (at least not yet).

Before you start saying, "Well, JB, isn't this just SEO?" Yes, this is the foundation of semantic SEO, which stresses creating helpful content and ensuring you have topical authority and depth, with the added nuance you need to develop content with AI systems in mind.

I may sound like a broken record, but topical authority has far more potential for LLMs than "highly optimized" pages focused on exact-match keyword phrases.
