# 2026 SEO Trends: Top Predictions from 20 Industry Experts

## Summary
Is AI killing your organic traffic and flipping SEO rules on their head? Learn how 20 experts are adapting for 2026 and shaping strategies AI platforms can't ignore.

## Extracted Body
If 2024 felt like a telenovela, 2025 was the spinoff nobody asked for.

Many websites suffered declining clicks due to AI search features, and just as we were getting used to AI Overviews (AIOs), Google rolled out AI Mode to compete with ChatGPT and other Large Language Models (LLMs).

Meanwhile, LLMs are launching their own browsers, which means we might need to start optimizing for SERPs in more channels.

So, how do you prioritize? What should you focus on when everything is in flux?

In this article, 20 SEO and content experts share their top predictions for 2026 to help you prepare for what’s next and build future-proof strategies.

By 2026, AI Mode will replace traditional SERPs as the dominant form of search. SEO will evolve into Relevance Engineering as conversational and personalized results become the norm.

Search behavior is no longer linear or universal. Instead of ten users seeing the same SERP, each user sees a uniquely generated experience tailored to their history, preferences, biases, and the AI’s interpretation of their intent. Traditional keyword tracking becomes obsolete in this environment.

To succeed, brands must move beyond static rankings and build omnichannel content networks that meet users wherever their “query fan-outs” lead.

In 2026, back-end SEO will be just as critical as front-end optimization. With the rise of agentic capabilities, brands will need to ensure that their APIs , databases, and content structures are accessible and usable by AI across smart devices like Google Nest, smart TVs, and others.

Users will expect AI to answer questions and act. Whether it’s booking appointments, ordering takeout, or playing a specific movie, AI assistants will need seamless access to structured data and executable actions.

Brands that don’t prepare their back-end systems will be invisible in this next phase of interaction.

In 2026, brand visibility in AI search results will hinge on trust. Earned media, such as social mentions, reviews, and high-quality backlinks, will play a key role in shaping how AI models and users perceive your brand.

LLMs prioritize content from trusted, credible sources. Brands consistently mentioned across the web are more likely to be surfaced in AI Overviews and LLM tools.

Sentiment around those mentions, whether positive or negative, directly influences how AI ranks and presents your brand, impacting visibility and user trust.

In 2026, brands that showcase how their products solve specific problems will win in AI search. With buyers using AI, content should focus on how your product solves a specific use case, rather than explaining problems.

Both B2B and B2C buyers increasingly expect AI tools to recommend the best product for their situation.

Buyers won’t waste time reviewing endless demos or trialing every option. Instead, they’ll prompt AI with highly specific queries like “landscape ideas for windy areas in full sun, Zone 10a California” or “project management tool with automated workflows for task dependencies.”

The brands that appear will be those that demonstrate value with visual proof, deep insight, and multi-format content.

Success Olagboye — Freelance Content Writer for B2B & B2C SaaS

In 2026, brands that publish across multiple content formats will enjoy more stable visibility in AI responses.

AI platforms like Perplexity are now pulling data from a broader set of channels, including social platforms like Instagram and Facebook.

They also prioritize formats that solve problems, such as calculators, templates, checklists, and interactive tools.

Brands that diversify their output are more likely to enjoy citation stability across various search experiences.

In 2026, brands will build content strategies around queries and topics that trigger Retrieval Augmented Generation in LLMs.

If you're aiming for visibility in LLM platforms, knowing which prompts trigger web searches for grounding is critical.

If no search is triggered, the response comes from the model itself, something you can’t influence until it’s retrained. However, when grounding happens, you have an opportunity to influence answers quickly.

Brands will focus on identifying and targeting grounded prompts to boost the return on content efforts.

Melissa Popp — VP of Content Strategy & Innovation at RicketyRoo

As more users turn to AI assistants for early-stage answers, TOFU content will shift from search visibility to model influence.

LLMs have become the new awareness engines. If your brand’s expertise isn’t reflected in their responses, you lose visibility before a search happens.

The brands that appear in AI answers will dominate the top of the funnel through education and earning citations from trusted sources. Ignoring this shift means losing your audience the moment curiosity sparks.

In 2026, top-performing content will be judged by its ability to deliver high Information Gain Rate while minimizing cognitive load. That means opening with concise, value-led summaries that deliver insight immediately.

A Surfer SEO report revealed that introductions are cited 43% more often in AI Overviews. When you deliver clarity and context upfront, you earn attention and, hopefully, citations.

In 2026, SEO will be driven by demand-led content that captures attention and builds affinity. Conversational AI is transforming search, and winning brands are those that create direct relationships with their audience without depending on traditional SERPs.

We’re entering a clickless future where AI platforms store chat history and answer follow-up questions in a single thread.

Organic traffic will continue to decline as Google and LLMs aim to deliver end-to-end search experiences without sending users to websites.

To stay visible, SEO must capture demand on external platforms and build deep affinity that allows you to serve content directly to users.

Chioma Anunobi — SEO Content Writer for Tech & Marketing Brands

In 2026, storytelling will move from a “nice to have” to a foundational content strategy that drives success.

While AI can generate summaries, it can’t replicate human experience or a unique perspective. Storytelling will be the differentiator that makes content memorable, shareable, and citation-worthy.

In an industry where 42% of marketers use AI to produce content, storytelling cuts through the noise and conveys value to humans and algorithms.

It’s how you create an emotional connection and brand recall, essential elements that allow you to serve content directly.

In 2026, search results will become more mixed, combining formats such as AI Overviews, shopping modules, webpage guides, and nested organic results that vary in position, sometimes appearing below position three or within other elements.

The great decoupling of 2025 , marked by sharp drops in organic clicks due to AI Overviews, changed how users interact with SERPs.

If AI-generated features stabilize lower on the page or become more complex, there’s still meaningful visibility and traffic to win.

The more we see these long-form or multi-step features, the more AI brand visibility becomes critical for SEO success.

The web will reach saturation with low-value, AI-generated content, forcing a radical shift in strategy to prioritize high-value, unique content.
