Without further ado, I present the Moz Top Creators of 2024, split into four categories.

The Helpful Content Update (HCU) focuses on brand demand rather than content quality. Sites with low brand recognition relative to their link profile are more likely to be penalized, emphasizing the need to balance authority and demand signals.

Don’t just focus on creating helpful content —work on building demand for your brand. Balancing content quality, links, and brand recognition is key to long-term SEO success.

Miriam identifies 11 fields in Google Business Profiles (GBPs) directly impacting local search rankings .

Ensure the primary category of your listing reflects your core offering. Use the additional nine categories to create a comprehensive picture of your business. Use tools like Moz Local to analyze local competitor categories for insights into optimizing your profile.

The Google API leaks revealed valuable insights into how Google's algorithms evaluate content, highlighting the importance of brand building, site authority, and topical backlinks for ranking.

Contributors stressed the impact of Navboost , Brand Authority, and user experience as key factors for boosting visibility. These elements highlight the importance of creating seamless, intuitive journeys that satisfy users and algorithms.

To succeed in Google Discover , leverage trending topics, build authority for your primary topics, and use great images and engaging titles to capture attention.

High-quality images and creating a “buzz” with traffic spikes are essential. Stunning visuals grab attention, and coordinated promotions can increase visiblity in Discover.

Combine SEO AI tools with human expertise to create content that meets E-E-A-T standards. Add personal experience, reliable research, and trustworthy information to make your content stand out.

Add personal stories and real-world examples to your AI content. People connect with people, and personal stories make your content relatable.

Keyword cannibalization occurs when multiple pages from your site compete for the same keyword, confusing search engines and lowering visibility. To fix it, identify conflicts, consolidate content, optimize internal linking, and create a hub-and-spoke structure.

Use a hub-and-spoke structure to resolve cannibalization. Create a central hub page for a topic and link related pages with thematic anchor text to consolidate authority and improve clarity for users and search engines.

GPT technology is changing how search works by encouraging users to include more detailed information in their queries. This shift makes searches longer and more specific, requiring SEOs to adapt their strategies for structured data and content.

Google’s filter bubbles encourage users to perform more detailed searches. To stay relevant, SEOs should focus on creating content that answers these detailed, specific queries.

Headless SEO focuses on creating flexible, reusable content that works across multiple platforms. Unlike a traditional CMS, it requires new strategies like content modeling and working with connected systems.

The music venue example perfectly explains content modeling—it details how to structure content like artists, performance dates, tickets, and merchandise to reuse efficiently across different platforms.

To optimize for AI overviews, use tools like the AI Overviews Visualizer to ensure your content is relevant. Write with authority, include quotes and statistics, and adapt strategies for specific queries like shopping and local searches to improve your chances of appearing in AI summaries.

Garrett’s highlight on volatility is critical—AI overviews are inconsistent, appearing in only 1% of queries (at the time the Whiteboard was filmed) and changing frequently. SEOs must cautiously weigh the risks of focusing on overviews against potential rewards like visibility and test strategies.

Building an SEO content architecture involves organizing your site into a clear structure with categories, landing pages, and blogs. This setup improves keyword rankings , drives conversions, and ensures scalability for future content.

Adriana explains using long-tail keywords and a keyword map. It simplifies content planning by aligning keywords, URLs, and page types, making your SEO strategy more focused and effective.

Relying on search traffic alone is no longer sustainable. Focus on creating high-quality, experience-driven content to build trust and authority . Diversify your traffic by leveraging platforms like YouTube and TikTok and expand across multiple channels to stay resilient.

With search becoming more unpredictable, focus on building traffic through other channels like social media, video platforms, and community spaces where your audience already spends time.

Large Language Models (LLMs) can automate tedious tasks, increase efficiency, and empower SEOs to focus on strategic initiatives. However, they require a thorough validation process to ensure accuracy and alignment with brand standards.

Aleyda emphasizes the value of prompt libraries to ensure team members work from the same source of truth when using LLMs . This approach ensures consistent quality and saves time for tasks like meta descriptions, bulk search intent analysis, and creating content briefs .

AI tools have transformed SEO, offering solutions for everything, including research, link building, content creation, and technical SEO . The key is to prioritize tools that align with your goals while maintaining strategic control over your outputs.

It is important to create your own prompts instead of relying on someone else’s. Tailored prompts ensure more personalized and high-quality outputs.

You must combine SEO and conversion rate optimization (CRO) to build a highly converting inbound machine. Driving traffic is critical, but optimizing for conversions ensures visitors take action, turning leads into revenue.

Andy emphasizes the importance of starting optimization at the bottom of the funnel . Fixing issues on your thank-you or contact pages before scaling traffic ensures every visitor has a clear conversion path.

Dr. Pete Meyers asserts that while AI search adoption is increasing, it’s not yet a direct replacement for Google, especially for commercial queries where the money lies. Tom underscores that Amazon is the bigger threat to Google’s dominance, with its search advertising revenue poised to surpass Google’s in a few years.

I loved Amanda’s spicy take — Executives often misunderstand attribution, focusing on unrealistic ROI measures instead of investment value. Dr. Pete reminds us that great content often plays a part in multi-touchpoint conversions—don’t undervalue its role in building trust and brand recall.

Interviewing Joshua was like eating cake. So much fun! From navigating challenges as an eBay drop shipper to building a thriving seven-figure SEO agency, Joshua’s story demonstrates that hard work, authenticity, and tuning into your unique background can lead to remarkable success.

I love how Joshua enticed cold email responses by offering a free video audit. People love freebies, especially when they are personalized to their unique problems. Joshua’s advice to build a strong team and stay focused on sales shows how delegation and smart prioritization can drive business growth.

Luke emphasizes that success isn’t just about making more money or landing big clients but designing a business that supports your desired lifestyle. It’s also important to set clear boundaries, price your services with confidence, and stay true to your expertise.

I love Luke’s method for determining rates: forget arbitrary revenue goals and base your pricing on your desired lifestyle. If you need only two working days a week to cover expenses and live comfortably, build your rates around that and treat everything else as a bonus.

Areej founded Women in Tech SEO to address the lack of visibility for women in technical SEO and to foster an inclusive environment where everyone can thrive. She explains how safe spaces empower women to build confidence, share knowledge, and create opportunities for personal and professional growth.

Areej's advice for starting a community is simple but powerful: Don't do it alone. Find others who share your passion— collaborate and build together. A trusted team brings diverse perspectives and makes it easier to brainstorm, share ideas, and grow something meaningful.

Aaron shares how focusing on e-commerce brands helped his agency land six-figure clients. Building persistence into your outreach, staying nimble as a small agency, and fostering strong client relationships are key to scaling successfully.

Aaron emphasizes persistence in client acquisition , sharing how it took 18 touchpoints to land one six-figure client. His creative use of physical mailers and personalized messaging is an excellent reminder that consistency and doing something unusual sometimes make all the difference.

Paul David proved that it’s possible to implement a 4-day work week and still be successful. He shared how investing in systems, clear communication with clients, and prioritizing employee well-being created a happier, more productive workplace.

I loved Paul’s “100-100-80” framework: 100% productivity, 100% pay, in 80% of the time. He explained how implementing the right systems and trust-building policies can make this work while maintaining profitability.
