## Extracted Article Passages
- Your product pages rank well in traditional search. Your category architecture is solid. Yet when a shopper asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google’s AI Overview for a buying recommendation in your category, your brand is nowhere. Competitors with weaker organic profiles are being cited, recommended, and preferred by AI systems, and your current agency cannot explain why.
- The problem is structural. Most agencies have rebranded traditional SEO with “AI” terminology without changing their methodology. This concern surfaces repeatedly on r/DigitalMarketing :
- “I keep seeing ‘AI SEO’ and ‘GEO’ on TikTok and Twitter but everyone explains it differently. Some say it’s just optimizing for AI answers. Some say it’s about structuring info for LLMs. Some say it’s basically BS and rebranded SEO.”
- The most consequential filter: does the agency use AI tools to execute traditional SEO, or does it engineer visibility across AI search surfaces? These are fundamentally different services solving different problems.
- AI SEO agencies for ecommerce, also called GEO or AIO agencies, specialize in engineering product and category visibility across AI-driven answer systems including Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, where buying recommendations are generated from structured signals rather than traditional ranking factors. A category page can rank for “wireless noise-cancelling headphones” because Googlebot indexed it. But if product specifications, review aggregates, and comparison attributes are locked inside JavaScript components that AI models cannot render, that page is invisible when ChatGPT generates a buying guide. The ranking and the citation are solved by different engineering.
- We evaluated AI search optimization agencies, sometimes called AIO or GEO agencies, against six criteria mapped to the specific challenges ecommerce brands face when optimizing for AI search surfaces. Verified outcome data for AI search optimization remains scarce industry-wide. Where agencies publish AI-specific case studies or citation metrics, we factored those in. Where they do not, we evaluated the depth and specificity of their documented methodology, ecommerce architecture expertise, and engagement model.
- We weighted AI search surface methodology and ecommerce architecture depth highest because these directly determine whether an agency can solve the specific problem this article addresses: product invisibility in AI-generated recommendations despite strong organic rankings.
- 1. AI Search Surface Methodology : Does the agency have a structured, repeatable process for improving visibility across AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, or have they relabeled traditional SEO?
- 2. Ecommerce Technical Architecture Depth : Can they diagnose and resolve JavaScript rendering failures, crawl waste from faceted navigation, and indexation bloat at catalog scale?
- 3. Structured Data and Entity Ecosystem Design : Do they build machine-extractable product entity systems, or just implement basic Product schema?
- 4. Engagement Model and Implementation Integration : Do they embed into development workflows, or deliver static audits?
- 5. AI Visibility Measurement and Ecommerce KPI Connection : Can they connect citation rates and surface presence to category traffic, product discovery, and assisted revenue?
- 6. Competitive AI Visibility Intelligence : Do they benchmark how AI platforms describe and prefer competitors, then close gaps?
- These six criteria work as an evaluation framework for any AI search optimization agency, including those not listed here.
- Disclosure: This guide is published by Onely, which appears at #1. We applied the same six criteria to our own services and to every competitor. Competitor information was independently verified where possible, and each entry’s strengths are presented alongside genuine limitations. If our ranking methodology doesn’t align with your priorities, the criteria above work as an evaluation framework for any agency.
- Methodology ratings: Documented = structured, repeatable AI search process with published methodology; Emerging = active development of AI-specific capabilities; Not Documented = no publicly verifiable AI search optimization methodology.
