## Extracted Article Passages
- Your product page ranks on page one for “men’s waterproof hiking boots size 11.” But when a shopper asks ChatGPT “what are the best waterproof hiking boots for wide feet?” or checks Perplexity for a comparison, your brand doesn’t appear. The AI system pulled from a competitor whose product data was cleaner, whose structured markup was consistent, and whose page actually rendered when the crawler visited.
- This is the gap most retail brands face now. Traditional SEO got products indexed. AI search optimization gets products recommended. Most agencies pitching “AI SEO” are simply using AI tools to write content faster, which helps their margins, not your AI visibility, rather than solving the structural problems that prevent ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews from reading and citing your product pages in the first place.
- Community discussions consistently reveal retailers spending months and thousands of dollars before discovering their agency can’t explain how AI systems actually extract product information, let alone fix the rendering, crawlability, and structured data issues blocking it.
- “Last year I hired an SEO agency here in Melbourne, thinking they’d help grow my small business and bring in steady leads. They promised the usual keyword research, backlinks, content, technical fixes but honestly, after months of paying them, I can’t see what they’ve actually done. Rankings are the same, traffic is flat, and every report they sent me was just screenshots from Google Analytics with no real explanation.”
- This guide ranks agencies by their ability to solve the specific technical and content problems that make retail catalogs invisible to AI platforms, not by brand size or marketing reputation.
- If your team hasn’t yet mapped how AI systems interact with your product pages, that’s normal – this capability gap exists at most retail organizations right now.
- We evaluated agencies against six criteria, weighted by what actually determines whether your products get surfaced in AI-powered search:
- Retail Catalog Technical Depth – Can they solve faceted navigation crawl waste, JavaScript rendering failures, and structured data inconsistency across thousands of SKUs? These structural problems are why AI systems skip entire product catalogs.
- AI Platform Optimization (not just AI tool usage) – Do they optimize your pages to be cited in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews (sometimes called GEO or generative engine optimization), or do they just use AI internally for efficiency? This distinction matters more than any other factor.
- Revenue-Tied Measurement – Do they connect work to revenue outcomes, or report rankings and impressions that don’t help you justify spend? Community research shows measurement is the single most discussed evaluation criterion.
- Implementation Scalability – Can recommendations actually be deployed across thousands of product pages via templates and dev workflows, or do they produce strategy documents that never get executed?
- Shopper Journey Understanding – Do they map how real buyers search and compare products, then structure content to match those patterns?
- Operational Quality – Communication consistency, team stability, and delivery reliability based on community feedback.
- These six factors – retail technical depth, AI platform optimization, revenue measurement, implementation scalability, shopper journey understanding, and operational quality – were weighted with technical and AI-specific capabilities scoring highest. We prioritize agencies that optimize FOR AI platforms over those that merely use AI tools internally.
- The distinction between these approaches surfaces repeatedly in practitioner discussions. As one experienced marketer on r/seogrowth explained:
- “In my experience, AI SEO is snake oil. I’ve committed myself to learning how SEO works and when it doesn’t work. If you do SEO well, it shows up on AI citation and in GPT/Claude conversations the same way a google search might. I had a client part ways to sign up with an AI SEO and I periodically check how its going with content and rankings to see if it’s much improved. It’s total snake oil. The AI SEO didn’t do much but post 4 shoddy, short AI blog posts a week. None of them rank.”
