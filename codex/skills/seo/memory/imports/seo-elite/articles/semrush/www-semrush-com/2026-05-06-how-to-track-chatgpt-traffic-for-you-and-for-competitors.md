## Why This Matters
Learn how to track ChatGPT traffic, benchmark against competitors, and connect AI visits to visibility signals.

## Extracted Article Passages
- ChatGPT is one of the most-used AI platforms on the web — and it’s starting to drive more web traffic.
- Semrush’s analysis of 17 months of clickstream data found that outbound referral traffic from ChatGPT grew 206% in 2025 .
- But how much traffic from ChatGPT are you getting? And are your competitors getting more of it?
- We asked SEO experts how they think about ChatGPT traffic and put together a guide on how to actually measure traffic from ChatGPT, so you can identify opportunities to improve.
- Visitors from ChatGPT arrive differently than visitors from organic search. By the time someone clicks through an AI response, they've already done plenty of research. They likely know more specifically what they want and why they want it.
- That changes the nature of the lead. Bek Drayton , Founder at BeKonstructive Marketing, sees it firsthand.
- From my personal experience, the leads we’re seeing come in from AI referral sources have pre-qualified themselves. They aren’t asking for a quote. They’re asking, “When can we get started?”
- In GA4, you can see which pages attracted visits from ChatGPT, how engaged users were once they arrived on your site, and whether they completed any conversions (these are called “key events” in GA4).
- Note: AI platforms don’t always pass referrer information, so some traffic from AI tools might appear as direct traffic.
- Click the drop-down at the top of the table and change it to “ Session source/medium .”
- Click “ + Add filter ” in the top left, then set the “Dimension” drop-down to “ Session source ,” set the “Match Type” drop-down to “ exactly matches ,” and click the box next to “chatgpt.com” in the “Value” drop-down.
- To see the exact pages visitors from ChatGPT went to, click the plus (+) icon next to the “Session source/medium” drop-down and select “ Landing page + query string .”
- Knowing which pages visitors from ChatGPT tend to go to on your site reveals what their intentions are.
- Once you’ve set up key events to track conversions in your GA4 account, you can check the “Engaged sessions,” “Key events,” and “Total revenue,” columns to measure how ChatGPT traffic interacts with your site.
- Checking conversions from ChatGPT traffic helps you answer the questions that matter most.
- When reporting to execs, Jimmy Rippon , Global Head of Search at Swoop Funding, recommends going one step further to quantify the value of ChatGPT traffic.
