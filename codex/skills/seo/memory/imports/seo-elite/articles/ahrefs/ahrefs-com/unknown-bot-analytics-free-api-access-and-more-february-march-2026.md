## Extracted Article Passages
- We’ve got 24 updates this month – from a brand new way to see how bots crawl your site to smarter AI workflows and more flexible reporting across tools.
- Meet Bot Analytics, a new tool that shows every bot crawling your website and the pages they hit, so you can protect your crawl budget from the wrong ones. It tracks bots across 12 categories like AI assistants, search engines, SEO tools, and social platforms.
- You can also toggle the “AI bots” filter to isolate AI traffic across all reports.
- Getting started requires a Cloudflare integration. Use Logpush for high-traffic sites or a Worker for simpler setups.
- Instead of writing prompts manually, you can now generate them with AI. Select your location and focus areas, like competitor comparisons or pricing pages, then review and refine the generated prompts before adding them.
- Prompts can now be grouped with tags, making it easier to analyze patterns across topics instead of reviewing them one by one.
- AI responses now include fanout queries, which show the additional searches models generate behind the scenes. Think of them as “hidden question” that often reveal useful subtopics to target.
- The Cited pages report has been expanded with new insights. A “pages by source” breakdown shows how citations are split between your pages, competitors, and others, with quick filters for each group.
- While the table will show which brands are mentioned on each cited page, along with page type classification and new DR, UR, and traffic metrics.
- The YouTube report now shows how often videos are cited in AI responses, broken down by chatbot. This makes it easier to spot which formats and creators are favored by AI.
- Custom prompts are now included in all of Ahrefs’ paid plans (except Starter), so you can start tracking your own prompts right away without needing a separate package.
- A new project-level navigation lets you switch between tools within the same project, making it faster to move between analysis and action.
- Content score and word count are now visible directly in the document list, so it’s easier to spot which articles need improvement. You can also batch export them to Google Docs, or delete and restore them in bulk.
- Instead of relying only on top SERP results, you can now add your own competitor URLs when creating a document. Benchmark against the pages that actually matter to your business.
- Pages can now be sent directly from Site Explorer’s Top Pages report into AI Content Helper, pre-filled with the page and its main keyword. This makes it faster to move from analysis to optimization.
- The Category filter helps narrow down keywords relevant to a specific niche, making keyword research faster and more focused.
