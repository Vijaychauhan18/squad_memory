# Core Concept
Patent Core analyzes `US2008005090A1`; the directly verifiable Google Patents record is the granted family member `US9009153B2`. The system identifies named entities from a user's context and turns them into weighted implicit queries.

## Official Patent Record
- Google Patents record used: `US9009153B2`
- Other version on the official page: `US20080005090A1`
- Title: `Systems and methods for identifying a named entity`
- Prior art date: `2004-03-31`
- Filing date: `2004-03-31`
- Publication date: `2015-04-14`
- Assignee: `Google LLC`
- Inventors: `Omar Habib Khan`, `Niniane Wang`, `Stephen R. Lawrence`
- Legal status: `Active`

## Practical Mechanism
- The system monitors user events such as reading, typing, messaging, or interacting with documents.
- It extracts named entities from those events, weights them, and automatically builds implicit search queries.
- Ranking and display are then adjusted with contextual relevance, click behavior, TF-IDF style weighting, and snippet generation.
- The patent also distinguishes boilerplate from substantive content and allows user-profile-driven personalization.

## SEO Reading
- Named entities are not decorative. They help systems understand what or who a page is really about.
- Context matters alongside entities. Good content connects entities to the user's task, not just to a keyword list.
- Engagement and snippet quality matter because the patent family explicitly learns from user interaction and result display behavior.

## What To Reuse
- Make key entities obvious in titles, headers, body copy, and structured data.
- Build pages that clearly separate unique main content from boilerplate noise.
- Improve snippet-worthiness by surfacing the key answer or identifying detail early.
- Treat entity optimization as context engineering, not entity stuffing.

## Small Details Worth Remembering
- The system works from event streams, not only static documents. That means context can come from reading, typing, messaging, or other activity.
- Named entities are weighted, and those weights influence which implicit queries get generated.
- The official family record includes snippet creation, result filtering, and click-through-based refinement, so this is not only an entity-extraction patent.
- The family explicitly distinguishes boilerplate from substantive content during retrieval preparation.

## E-E-A-T And Audit Lens
- Strong entities help Google understand who is speaking, what is being discussed, and why the page is relevant in context.
- Entity clarity supports expertise audits because subject-matter focus becomes more legible to the system.
- Boilerplate-heavy templates can drown out the actual expertise signal of a page.

## Real Audit Questions
- Are the main people, organizations, products, or concepts on the page explicitly named and reinforced?
- Does the page expose those entities in titles, headers, schema, and descriptive copy?
- Is the unique content easy to separate from template chrome, navigation, and repeated blocks?
- Would a snippet system find the real answer fast, or mostly find scaffolding?

## Caveat
The Patent Core article uses the application number in the title, while the official Google Patents page is the granted family member. Reuse the mechanism, not the numbering mismatch.
