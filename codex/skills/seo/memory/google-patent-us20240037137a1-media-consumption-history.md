# Google Patent US20240037137A1 — Media Consumption History

## What it covers
Stores cross-platform consumption signals (purchases, streaming, social, browsing, listening) in a unified database and uses them to re-rank knowledge card results when users query entities such as artists, products, films, or brands. Engagement history across non-search surfaces directly informs entity search ranking.

## Core mechanism
1. Cross-platform consumption events logged (YouTube watches, Play purchases, Maps visits, Drive access, etc.)
2. Unified consumption history stored per user
3. When user queries an entity, consumption history used to re-rank knowledge card results
4. Entity associations strengthened by repeated cross-platform engagement

## SEO signals
- **Cross-surface engagement strengthens entity association** — YouTube videos, Maps listings, and Google Business interactions all feed the same entity ranking signal
- **Brand entity signals are multi-platform** — a brand with strong YouTube, Maps, and Shopping presence has compounding entity authority vs a web-only brand
- **Knowledge card ranking is personalised** — for logged-in users, entity results reflect their consumption history, not just global signals

## Practitioner implication
For brands: invest in the full Google entity surface (YouTube, Google Business, Shopping, Maps) not just organic search. Each interaction platform feeds the entity consumption history that re-ranks knowledge cards. This is why entity SEO and brand authority work across Google products, not just Search.
