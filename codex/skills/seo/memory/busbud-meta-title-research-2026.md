# Busbud Meta Title and Description Research

## Exact Evidence Window
- Primary landing-page export basis: 2026-04-13
- Main strategy memo: 2026-04-16
- Competitor meta research: 2026-04-17
- PRD and reconfirmation pass: 2026-04-21

## Core Diagnosis
Busbud's metadata issue is a template-system problem. The live formulas combine route or entity intent with dynamic prices, refund language, promo framing, and long brand endings. That creates lower CTR potential, higher title-rewrite risk, and weaker alignment with cleaner on-page H1s.

## Highest-Signal Numbers
- Relevant landing-page rows in scope: `27,161`
- Clicks across those rows: `531,478`
- Impressions across those rows: `40,093,661`
- Titles over 60 characters: `27,161 / 27,161`
- Titles including live price or currency: `22,484`
- Promo-heavy titles: `24,983`

## Template Priorities
- `route`: `437,969` clicks, `82.4%` of clicks in scope. This is the main business case.
- `POI-route`: `41,430` clicks, `7.8%`.
- `station`: `28,223` clicks, `5.3%`.
- `city`: `15,360` clicks, `2.9%`.
- `train-route`: `7,844` clicks, `1.5%`, but much larger impression share than click share, which signals underperformance.

## Live Review Pattern
The 56-URL live sample reconfirmed:
- `56 / 56` titles too long
- `34 / 56` descriptions too long
- `24 / 56` titles with live price or currency
- `40 / 56` promo-heavy titles
- `56 / 56` title and H1 mismatch pattern

## What This Means Operationally
- Remove live price and currency from titles.
- Remove refund and cancellation wording from titles.
- Keep titles query-led and template-specific.
- Move commercial hooks into the meta description or on-page blocks.
- Make H1 and title say the same thing more clearly so Google has less reason to rewrite.

## Best Rollout Order
1. `route`
2. `station`
3. `POI-route`
4. `train-route`
5. `city`, `country`, `POI`

## Competitor Readout To Reuse
- On route and train pages, Busbud competes against a mixed field: some commercial aggregators also use price-led titles, but cleaner operator titles still exist and often look more stable.
- On station and POI-route queries, official transit and airport pages are materially cleaner, more entity-led, and less promotional. That means Busbud should not benchmark those templates against the noisiest aggregator copy.

## Recommended Title Direction
- Route: `Bus from {Origin} to {Destination} | Tickets & Timetables | Busbud`
- Train-route: `Train from {Origin} to {Destination} | Tickets & Schedules | Busbud`
- Station: `{Station Name}, {City} | Bus Tickets, Schedules & Destinations | Busbud`
- POI-route: `Bus from {Origin} to {POI Short Name} | Tickets & Schedules | Busbud`
- City: `Bus Tickets to {City} | Routes & Operators | Busbud`

## Metrics To Reuse In Future Checks
- CTR change by template on stable-ranking query groups
- Share of titles over target length
- Share of titles with price, currency, refund, or savings language
- Share of pages where title and H1 drift materially
- Click lift on top operator and route clusters
