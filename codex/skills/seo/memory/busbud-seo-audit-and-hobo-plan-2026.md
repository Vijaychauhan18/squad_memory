# Busbud SEO Audit and Hobo Action Plan

## Core Diagnosis
Busbud is not failing because Google cannot crawl or index the site. The site has real authority, strong international structure, and visible rich-result presence. The repeatable upside comes from improving click efficiency, trust surfacing, and template quality on high-impression pages.

## What Was Already Working
- Crawlability and indexation looked broadly healthy in the audit sample.
- International architecture and hreflang coverage were strengths.
- Rich-result presence already existed through product and review snippets.
- South Africa, English global, Canada, and Spanish LATAM were standout clusters.

## Main Repeatable Problem
The biggest upside is on pages ranking in positions roughly `4-8` with lots of impressions but weak CTR. In plain English: Busbud often gets seen, but too many templates do not win the click strongly enough.

## Hobo-System Readout To Reuse
- Domain-level strength is already solid.
- Faster wins are not primarily from more authority building.
- The first systems to improve are:
  1. `Goldmine`
  2. `CRAPS`
  3. `Q* / Site_Quality`
  4. `T*`

## What Those Systems Mean In Busbud Context
- `Goldmine`: SERP packaging is underperforming because titles and snippets are too long, brittle, or promo-heavy.
- `CRAPS`: pages rank but may under-satisfy or under-convert impressions into clicks.
- `Q* / Site_Quality`: Busbud has real authority, but many templates do not expose trust and machine-readable quality strongly enough.
- `T*`: operator and route pages often do not cover the full user-needs cluster tightly enough.

## Practical Fixes To Reuse
- Rewrite titles and descriptions on operator and route pages.
- Add top-of-page answer blocks on operator pages.
- Surface booking, baggage, refund, payment, route coverage, and FAQ content more clearly.
- Clean up malformed or inconsistent schema on key templates.
- Reduce internal-link noise where template pages are overloaded.
- Fix reporting logic when tools such as Lumar create false-positive panic.

## Priority Rule
- Start with operator pages where demand and CTR gaps are clearest.
- Then fix route-title logic and route-page information packaging.
- Treat thin, low-demand long-tail pages as prioritization candidates, not universal rescue targets.
